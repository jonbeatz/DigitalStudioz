## IMPORTANT NOTES ABOUT ALL SCANNERS IN THIS CATEGORY

1. **All are "interpolated" resolution** - The native sensor in these budget scanners is typically 5-14MP. The 22/24/25MP claims are achieved through software interpolation (upscaling). Don't expect true 24MP DSLR-scanned quality.

2. **All use USB for computer connection** - Every scanner listed connects to PC/Mac via USB cable. None REQUIRE an SD card (they all have internal memory + USB transfer option). However, SD cards are convenient for batch scanning.

3. **Image quality is roughly equivalent** - At this price point ($75-150), all these scanners use similar internal hardware. The differences are primarily in display size, build quality, included accessories, and customer support.

4. **Super 8 support means 8mm movie film** - These scan individual frames from Super 8/8mm movie film strips, not entire reels at once. It's frame-by-frame.

5. **Plustek and Epson options** - True dedicated USB film scanners from Plustek (OpticFilm series) start at $350+. Epson flatbed scanners (V600) that do film are $250+. These are out of the $75-150 budget but offer significantly better quality if budget can stretch.
